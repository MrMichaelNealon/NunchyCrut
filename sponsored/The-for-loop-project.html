We are planning the future of humanity so that you don't have to

Every day, humans spend an estimated total of 33 million hours writing for loops.

That is over a billion man-hours per year, wasted! At The for society we believe in pre-emptive action and so we embarked on the for project.

The vision

To build an extensive collection of pre-written for loops that can be copied and pasted into any project!

Naturally, this is a mammoth undertaking, the for society was founded in 1943 and the for project itself began in 1946 when the for society recruited ex-nazi scientists to get the project off the ground.

Since then the team have worked tirelessly to bring us every possible for loop we can imagine in multiple languages, churning out as many as two or three loops in a week!

Meet chairman Whittaker Smix

An interview with the groups CEO and founder, one Whittaker Smix proved insightful when it came to understanding their philosophy:

"The real work began in the early 50's when one of our engineering team knocked out a succession of for loops in c and JavaScript.

He had compiled a collection of 10 pre-written for loops in both languages. So one loop in c that would iterate 0 through 1, another that iterated 0 through 2, etc...all that way up to a loop that would do 0 through 10. So we had 20 for loops in our database, it was a great start!

Of course we had merely scratched the surface, we needed to have loops that went from 0 to a lot higher than 10! We also had to think about loops that don't even begin with zero! Can you imagine? "

Smix and his cohort worked tirelessly from the 50s right through to the present day and they have compiled a vast collection of for loops that are free for anyone to use.

API

No more will you have to manually type out a for loop, simply use the amazing for loop API to grab a pre-written for loop and away you go!

The API now boasts a collection of approximately 300 loops available in c, JavaScript and php!

"We are still busy writing loops but it is time consuming. If anyone is willing to contribute that would be most welcome!

It's ironic to think that I began this project 80 years ago in the hopes that one day I would never have to write another for loop - and I've subsequently spent my life doing exactly that!"

He rambled.
