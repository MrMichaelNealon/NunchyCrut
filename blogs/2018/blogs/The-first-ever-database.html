The first ever database

Having made quite a name for himself so early in his career, Rudyard decided to expand his empire by branching out into other areas.

"I wanted to modify my pop-up contraption in such a way that it could change the advert. This way I could have one for say...a butcher, one for a baker, I could maybe even have one for a candlestick maker!

This dynamic model would require a means by which I could store information and quickly change the contents of the pop-up on the fly...or should I say on the bee? Ha ha ha!"


After racking his brains for a few weeks, Rudyard came up with the an ingenious solution, he would store the data in bee hives and use the bees themselves as a sort of transportation-twixed rendering protocol...

"I had acquired a beehive as a gift from my niece, it suddenly struck me that I could train bees to store honey and use the honey to quickly render the advertisment onto the face of my pop-up.

I removed a thin slither of bee hive, it had all them hexagons and honey on it, I got stang multiple times but I was soon friends with the bees and they responded to my instructions. I spent some weeks training the bees to store the honey in the little hexagons then retrieve it, and smear it on the face of the pop-up to construct a dynamic advert - it worked!

An early database, circa 1803.


Rock star

Now, with his new database feature, Rudyard set to work. He strapped the thin slither of bee hive to his contraption and set up a test, deploying his device in a field just outside Birmingham.

Sure that his device would be a huge success, he tucked in to some Mr Kipling bramley apple tarts and a bottle of Frijj strawberry milkshake and waited to become a rock star.

"I waited patiently for the new pop-up to be triggered, it took a day or two but soon I got word that it had been sprung, and it had worked!

Unfortunately, as with the previous release there was a bug and the unwitting user was stang in the face multiple times and died due to an allergic reaction. I was immediately put on death row!


Rudyard wa again awaiting execution. But, as ever - he was determined not to let it get him down, he continued to work on a solution to his problem while on death row for the second time in his career.

Genetics

While in prison, Rudyard had concluded that the best solution would be to breed a species of bee that doesn't feature a stinger, but in the end he decided instead that it'd be easier to simply snap the stingers off of the bees that he already had.

After being in prison for some hours, Rudyard was granted a royal pardon when a sympathetic Queen learnt that he too had been stang by the bees - Rudyard was again a free man.

"I immediately set to work, snapping the stingers off of all my bees, and I got stang plenty of times in the process of doing so.

After several painstaking hours I had a hive full of stingless bees. I threw together another prototype and again deployed, this time in a forest near Slough.
